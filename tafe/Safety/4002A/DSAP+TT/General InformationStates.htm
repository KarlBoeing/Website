General Information on Safety Legislation in Australian States

Australian Safety and Compensation Council
lead and coordinate Australia's national effort to:
·         promote best practice in occupational health and safety (OHS)
·         improve workers' compensation arrangements
·         improve rehabilitation and return to work of injured workers
http://www.ascc.gov.au/

Wester Australia

WorkSafe is a division of the Department of Commerce, the Western Australian State Government agency responsible for the administration of the Occupational Safety and Health Act 1984. 

The principal objective of the Occupational Safety and Health Act 1984 is to promote and secure the safety and health of persons in the workplace.

Safety is a concern in all Australian states and the Occupational Safety and Health Act & regulations are fairly similar to the western Australian one.

http://www.docep.wa.gov.au/WorkSafe/

New South Wales Work
Cover NSW promotes workplace health and safety, and provides a workers compensation system for the employers and workers of.

Victoria
WorkSafe Victoria is working with the community to deliver outstanding workplace safety, together with quality care and insurance protection to employers and workers. http://www.workcover.vic.gov.au/wps/wcm/connect/WorkSafe

Queensland
Workplace Health and Safety is a subdivision of the Department of Employment and Industrial Relations which administers all health and safety aspects
http://www.deir.qld.gov.au/workplace/index.htm

South Australia
SafeWork SA is responsible for administering occupational health, safety and welfare and industrial relations laws in South Australia.
http://www.safework.sa.gov.au/

Australian Capital Territory
ACT WorkCover is the government agency responsible for the administration of the Occupational Health and Safety Act 1989 , Dangerous Substances Act 2004, Scaffolding and Lifts Act 1912, and Machinery Act 1949.
http://www.ors.act.gov.au/workcover/WebPages/WorkSafe/ohs.htm

Northern Territory
NT WorkSafe is the administrative and regulatory arm of the Northern Territory Work Health Authority - the statutory body established under the Workplace Health and Safety Act. http://www.worksafe.nt.gov.au/

Tasmanian
Part of the duties of Workplace Standards Tasmania is to administer the legislation which includes promoting and encouraging safety in the workplace and also ensuring compliance with the legislation.
http://www.wst.tas.gov.au/safety_comply;

HARMONISING OSH LEGISLATION
As every State has its own Occupational Safety & Health laws. Despite the commonality, there remain differences between jurisdictions and matters in OSH legislation.
Therefore the Australian Government has asked for a 'National Review into Model Occupational Health and Safety (OSH) Law'.
