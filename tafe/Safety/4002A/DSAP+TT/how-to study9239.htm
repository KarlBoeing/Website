How to study this Unit

This Unit is computer based and can be studied in three modes

  1. internally in Class if you have no Internet access or not a computer ,
  2.  externally at home (work) if Internet access is available, and
  3. as a combination of the above.
Students enrolled in this unit don't need to come to every class but must access the course every week for 1.5 hours.
Students failing to do this may be taken off the course and access to the course is then denied. You need to re-enrol!

Class attendance is not required. However, if you study at home or work and need any clarification on an issue you should attend class and discuss the matter with your lecturer. From time to time there may be a compulsory class attendance required. The date for the compulsory attendance will be announced on the home page of the web site.

Guidance - Working through the Unit

  1. The first section gives you the general idea of the course CPCCBC4002A (W9239). It contains general instructions and the Learning Plan for this Unit (time table and assessment requirements). Please read 'How to study this Unit' thoroughly to make sure you understand how this unit will be delivered. Click on all links and read through the text provided in this section. You may briefly browse to all sections of the unit to get familiar with the structure of the course.

    The first eight weeks you'll concentrate on the online quizzes. By doing the quizzes numerous times you'll get to know the OSH-Act and the OSH-Regultions, That's all what you doing in the first eight weeks.

  2. The second section deals with Online Quizzes. These quizzes provide you with the underpinning knowledge for this Unit. This section will run for nine (9) weeks and finished with an online assessment quiz, which test your knowledge of the Occupational Safety & Health Legislation. There are three (3) online practice quizzes [Practice Quiz 1 (10 question), Practice Quiz 2 (20 questions) and a Final Practice Quiz (30 questions)]. You should do each quiz at least three times but you can do them as often as you want. Please make sure you submit the quiz because if you don't do it you can't access them anymore.

    The online assessment is in Week 9. If you are familiar with the quizzes at an earlier time you may move on to the elements. But having successful completed the online assessment you must then proceed to the study of Element 1.
  3. In the third section (Element 1) you will determine areas of potential risk in the building and construction workplace. You will identify hazards that have the potential to cause harm (injury, disease, death, property and equipment damage). Analysing statistical data will give an indication of the major safety risks in the workplace. Any risks identified a in your workplace must be controlled. There is a specific order of control that you must know of. The principles of risk management need to be understood. If you finished the study of Element 1 and read all the information provided in this section you should have an understanding of the subject matter. If you have a good understanding of the element matter then you should proceed to Element 2.

    Don't forget to do the self test!
  4. In the fourth section (Element 2) you need to inspect and report on areas of specific risk. This element has a formative assessment a 'Workplace Inspection Report'. Students who are not working on a construction site to carry out a hazard inspection may use the college ground/building for your hazard/risk identification assessment. More information is provided in Element 2 document. Other workplace inspection arrangements need to be discussed with your lecturer. A link to a safety inspection template (doc-file and pdf-file) for the college site safety inspection is provided.
    Companies have usually readymade checklists for hazard identification of specific workplaces. A link to some checklist examples can be found in the Element 2 document.
    Read all documents listed in this section to ensure your have a good grasp of the subject matter. If you have read through all documents in this element then you should proceed to Element 3.

    Don't forget to do the self test!
  5. The fifth section (Element 3) deals with advise on implementation of control measures at the building and construction workplace. This is a continuation of the formative assessment hazard and risk identification that has been carried out in the previous element. Students must complete a form by describing hazard and your recommended corrective action.Your inspection should contain at least one hazard that needs to reported. You will make recommendations from findings of your inspection report and discuss appropriate control measurements. A link to a report template (doc-file and pdf-file) is provided. (If you don't want to use this form you may download any hazard report form from the Internet.

    A Job Safety Analysis (JSA) is the systematic identification of potential hazards in the work place by personnel as a first step to controlling the possible risks involved. In a JSA, each basic step of the job is examined to identify potential hazards and decide on the safest way to do a job. There is an JSA exercise for three to five students who like to work together. If you not attending class use the Mail to communicate with each other.

    6. Reading all documents in this section will assist you to develop a broad understanding of the subject covered. You should now proceed to section six to study Element 4.

    Don't forget to do the self test!
  6. The sixth section (Element 4) deal with establishing and reviewing communications and educational programs. Procedures need to be set out for communication and information specific to the building and construction industry and in accordance with statutory requirements. Consultation with employees should be made available and current health and safety information needs to be discussed.

    Reading all documents in this unit will assist you to develop a broad understanding of the Occupational Safety and Health requirements

    Don't forget to do the self test!
Having worked through all elements you should be able to pass your final assessment.
