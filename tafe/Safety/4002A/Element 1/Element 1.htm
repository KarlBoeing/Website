Element 1

Determine areas of potential risk in the building and construction workplace.

 Performance criteria

1.1 Specific risks for the range of occupations in the workplace are identified and prioritised.
1.2 Construction site safety is evaluated and construction hazards and potential risk areas are identified in accordance with legislative requirements for OHS and company policies.
1.3 Hazards are identified and prioritised and required approaches to remediation are documented.


There are many risks on a building site, which need to be identified not only by the employer but also by workers in the building industry. A risk is a likelihood or probability that a hazardous event, with a given outcome or consequence, will occur. To ensure workers are able to identify hazards and assess risks they need to be familiar with the Occupational Safety & Health Act and Regulations.

There are quizzes which you should attempt. They make you familiar with the safety legislation and prepare your for assessment quizzes.

Hazard identification

To identity a hazard means looking for those things (e.g. condition, situation, practice, behaviour etc.) at the workplace that have the potential to cause harm (injury, disease, death, property and equipment damage).

The Worksafe statistics would provide you with the necessary information to address the matter in Element 1. Please investigate Worksafe statistics to learn about specific risks. The next step would be to evaluate construction hazards, prioritise them and compile a OSH-Policy document. (see Regulation 3.1)

Compared to other industries, the construction industry has a consistently higher than average rate of Lost Time Injuries and Diseases (LTI/Ds1), as shown in the opposite Graph 1

Although the figures relate to the industry as a whole, they are relevant to those grouped under either 'general construction' or 'construction trade services'.



The occupations that figure most prominently in the statistics for the industry are tradesperson and related workers, labourers and related workers, and production and transport workers.
Even if your occupation is not on the graph this does not mean your occupation has not recorded any lost time injuries or disease, as it may be covered under "other occupations". The largest category tradesperson and related workers includes carpenters, plasters, tilers, bricklayers, stonemasons and painters.
