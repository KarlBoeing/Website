Element 4

Establish and review communications and educational programs..

 Performance criteria

4.1 Effective strategies for communicating occupational health and safety policy and practice are determined in consultation with appropriate personnel.
4.2 Communication strategies and educational programs specific to the building and construction industry and in accordance with statutory requirements and best practice are established.
4.3 The effectiveness of the communication and educational programs are reviewed.


Communication strategies

Know the workforce and decide which are the most effective strategies for communicating OS&H requirements

Make a plan

Procedures need to be set out for communication and information. Consultation with employees should be made available and current health and safety information needs to be discussed.

Communicate for understanding

Appropriate forms of communication are direct face-to-face communication and demonstration. Use plain language Information as clear as possible. Displayed safety signs in appropriate form and teach employees the meaning of all signs. Written material often requires translation because of employee's lack of the English language. Provide interpreter services. Make sure employees understand the instructions and check that employees have understood training instruction and information.

Provide training

Employees need to undertake induction training incorporating general health and safety rights and responsibilities. Common workplace terms should be taught. Make sure that employees who do not speak English can understand and discuss the content.

Assess current health and safety information

a) The first step in improving communications in the workplace is to examine the information that is      currently available to employees.

b) In multilingual workplaces, employers must ensure that all heath and safety information is available      to staff either in their preferred language or in a form that they can understand. Failure to provide this      information in an appropriate form could place employees at risk.

c) Common examples of health and safety information include:

       the company's health and safety policy
       the location and nature of hazards in the workplace
       procedures for the safe operation, use, maintenance and replacement of protective
          equipment
       injury and incident reporting procedures
       consultation structures (eg HSRs, designated work groups, management contacts and
          meeting schedules)
       procedures for resolving health and safety issues
       emergency and first aid procedures
       safety signs and symbols.

Communication with employees

    Think about what you want to say and the simplest way to say it

     Use plain, simple language
     Be active: talk, do demonstrations and use charts, pictures and diagrams
     Speak slowly and clearly using short, complete sentences
     Use plain language written materials to support discussion
     Use audio or audiovisual material in appropriate languages
     Provide written information in appropriate forms and languages
     Use consistent terms and symbols
     Make information readily accessible to all employees
     Provide interpreter services
     Allow time for discussion and consideration
     Check for understanding
