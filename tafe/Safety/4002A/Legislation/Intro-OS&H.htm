Occupational Safety & Health Legislation

The content of this page covers the required underpinning knowledge. Without an understanding of the Occupational Safety and Health legislation you cannot successful complete this Unit. This page helps you to access the relevant information on Occupational Safety and Health legislation.

First of all you need to study the makeup of the Act and Regulations in greater detail before you attempt quizzes. The quizzes will ask you specific questions about the OS&H Act and Regulations and you will not be able to answer questions if you do not know the nitty gritty of the Act & Regulations. Please study the contents of the Act as well as the Regulations (click the links contents page below). It will be of great benefit if you would make a copy of the contents pages. In more than one cases you may answer quiz questions with the information provided in the contents pages. If this is not possible search for the specific section of the Act or Regulations.

Students who find it complicated to work with downloaded Act and Regulations (pdf-doc) on the taskbar encouraged to buy a hardcopy of the Act and Regulations from the State Law Publisher. (See contact details below)

Access also the other publication on the WorkSafe site (Code of Practice and Guidance Notes) to get an overview of all aspects of safety and health.
General Information

To identify hazards in the workplace you need to become familiar with the safety legislation. There are important documents that you must know:

  • Occupational Safety & Health Act 1984
  • Occupational Safety & Health Regulations 1996
  • Codes of Practice
  • Guidance notes (States & National)
  • Australian Standards (mentioned in the Regulations)

    • Please note that each Australian state has its own Occupational Safety & Health legislation. Despite the commonality, there remain differences between jurisdictions and matters in OSH legislation (see link below).

    The Act consist of eight parts:
  • Part I — Preliminary
  • Part II — Commission for Occupational Safety and Health
  • Part III—General provisions relating to occupational safety and health
  • Part IV — Safety and health representatives and committees
  • Part V — Inspectors
  • Part VI — Improvement and prohibition notices
    • Part VIA — Safety and health magistrates
    Part VIB — Occupational Safety and Health Tribunal
  • Part VII — Legal proceedings
  • Part VIII — Miscellaneous

    • Part VIII (Section 60) refers to the Regulations in which all matters are described that are specified in the Act

    If you want to get a view of the OS&H Act contents page you should click on the link to see the extent of the Act in greater detail.

    The Regulations is made up of seven parts:
  • Part 1 — Preliminary and interpretation
  • Part 2 — General and Health
  • Part 3 — Workplace safety requirements
  • Part 4 — Plant
  • Part 5 — Hazardous substances
  • Part 6 — Performance of high risk work
  • Part 7 — Repeal, savings and transitional
    • including Schedules 1 to 6

    If you want to get a view of the OS&H Regulations contents page you should click on the link to see the extent of the Regulations in greater detail.

    You'll get familiar with the the Act and Regulations if you attempt quizzes as soon as possible. Anyway the quizzes will make you familiar with all matters described in the Act and Regulations.

    Interrelationship of the legislation

    The Act provides a framework where consultation, cooperation, regulations, codes of practice, workplace standards and procedures to resolve issues support the general duty of care. The general duty of care is the guiding principle for all other parts of the Act.


    The Occupational Safety and Health Act sets objectives to promote and improve occupational safety and health standards. The Act sets out broad duties and is supported by more detailed requirements in the Occupational Safety and Health Regulations. The legislation is further supported by guidance material such as approved Codes of Practice. This legislative framework is depicted below.




    The Act is a State legislation and all other Australian States have similar legislation.


    The Occupational Safety and Health Regulations, made under the Act, describe some of the requirements, which apply to specific work situations.
    Not complying with the regulation may result in a penalty payment.




    Code of  Practice and Guidance Notes themselves are not legally binding but they provide you with specific and detailed information that is not covered in the regulations.

    How to answer quizzes and assessments

     All quizz questions and assessment questions have one or two answer boxes. Most important is how you type your answers in the boxes. Refer to a typical question below for a correct input into the boxes:

    In the first box a choice of a, b, c, or d must be entered. This can be done in lower or upper case.

    In the second box a Regulation or an Act number must be entered.
    Example - r3.112 or a57 (R3.112 or A56 is also acceptable)

    Note: - No space between letter and numbers!

    Do not forget to save your answer! You can modify the answer later if you wish but do save your answer again.

    The correct input of your answers is very important. A failure to do so will mark your answer wrong although it could be right.

    Don't attempt a quiz if you aren't prepared for it. Make sure you allowed sufficient time for completion of the quiz before you start the quiz.

    You can access each quiz four (5) times. A certain time limit is set to answer a quiz. Watch the Time remaining in the upper right corner. Make sure you click the "Finish" button otherwise you won't be able to access the quiz again. Note your results & time and compare this with your next attempt. Your next attempt should give you a higher score and a better time.

    Assessments*

    The question for the assessments are similar to the questions in the quizzes. Please note how much time is allocated for the assessment. Make sure you finish all question on time. Save each question as soon as you have answer it. If you modify an answer don't forget to press the "Save" button again. When you have answered all questions you must click the "Finish" button.
    *) Assessment 1 will test the legislative requirements. It is an online quiz assessment like the practise quizzes. When you finish the quiz submit it and then view your score. A 75% is required to pass. Students who have a score between 65 to 74% may resit the test. A score less than 65% require a re-enrol (also applicable for Assessment 3).
    *) Assessment 2 is a paper base workplace inspection and hazard report. Element 1 and 2 provides you with the necessary information. Please see your lecturer if you have any query regarding this formative assessment.
    *) Assessment 3 is an online assessment that covers the legislative requirements as well as the subject matter of all elements. Please make sure you have done all 'self tests' questions as they are an integral part of this assessment.
