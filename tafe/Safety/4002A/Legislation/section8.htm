Risks identification

The probability of injury or harm occurring can be assessed by looking at past records, including accidents, "near miss" incidents or previous first aid requirements.

The consequences of the exposure to identified hazards can range from very minor to potentially fatal injuries or harm.

Risk assessment is the process of evaluating the probability and consequence of injury or illness arising from exposure to identified hazards.

Identify the regulatory requirements of the OS&H Legislation to scaffolding and excavation (trenches):

a) for scaffolding in the sketch.
b) for trenches* in the sketches.

*Additional information relevant to trenches can be found in the "Code of Practice: Excavation".

  1. Look for safety issues in the scaffolding sketch. Click on those areas in the sketch to find 'Hazard 1 to 10'. The next step is to identify the appropriate regulation numbers for all hazards by using Regulations on-line or hardcopy. Place the appropriate regulation number in Reg-boxes and make sure you get it right . Click  Check  to check your answer [you have three (3) attempts].

  2. Identify regulation numbers for all trench sketches by using Regulations on-line or hardcopy. For each sketch type in the Reg-boxes the appropriate regulation number [you have three (3) attempts to get it right]. Click  Check  to check your answer.
