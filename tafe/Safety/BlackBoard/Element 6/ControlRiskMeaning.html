Develop and manage Safety Plan for Construction projects

Extract from RTA design and manage safe projects
(the complete guide covers a range of construction activities)

Concept development phase

The Project Manager must ensure that all factors that may adversely affect health and safety are identified and given appropriate consideration at the route selection phase by designers and engineers.

Crucial decisions are made during route selection which determines major features of the facility to be constructed and these decisions will likely have significant impact on the management of health and safety during construction and subsequent maintenance.

Input from the specialist OHS Adviser and relevant field staff should assist in the consideration of factors affecting health and safety at this phase. If health and safety issues cannot be effectively addressed at this phase then they the concept development phase that may have bearing upon the OHS of the project.

Detailed design phase

All OHS hazards identified during concept design that are unable to be controlled must be considered during the detailed design phase. The OHS Development Plan for the project shall detail these hazards and also the control measures identified to reduce risk of injury both in terms of construction and maintenance phases of the project.

Detailed designers are responsible to not only address the hazards identified during the concept phase, but also to identify those specific OHS hazards resulting from the design of the projects components.

Where such hazards are unable to be controlled, such hazards shall form the basis of project specific OHS hazards to be detailed within OHS contract specifications G21/22 and further detailed within the OHS Development Plan for the benefit of maintenance phase of the project. Construction phase

The construction phase of a project brings to life many of the hazards identified in both the concept and detailed design phases. This phase will also introduce many new hazards as site-specific hazards are exposed and construction methodology is implemented.

The risk management processes utilised and especially the control measures implemented will not only affect the OHS of the construction phase, but may also filter into the maintenance phase of the project. Especially where the design of the project components is altered during construction.

Maintenance phase

OHS hazards within the maintenance phase of a project are a result of the risk management processes leading up to handover. All OHS hazards identifiable in the planning stages of development projects should be detailed within Project Briefs.
