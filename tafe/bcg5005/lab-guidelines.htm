Soil Mechanics Lab Report Guidelines

 

Laboratory Reports:

A report need to be submitted for each test we do in the laboratory. Some of the experiments may be carried out as group activity. In this case, only one report per group, signed by all individual group members, needs to be submitted. The group as a whole is responsible for ensuring that work is fairly distributed among group members. (You should work through each problem together an have an understanding of the experiment.))

Write the reports as soon as possible after performing the experiment. Group members will be orally assessed to verify that the competencies have been achieved.

A word processor should be use to prepare the reports. The font is to be 12 point Times Roman or Aerial. Margins should be one inch on top, bottom and right hand side and 1.5 inch on left hand side.

Be professional in your presentation. Keep the content technically accurate and use an appropriate method to present the relevant data.

Charts and graphs can be hand drawn in ink if necessary. Some reports will be short, some will be longer. Try not to make them long while at the same time do not leave out information to make them short.

Lab reports must be turned in at the beginning of class the day they are due (usually the following week, unless noted otherwise). The submitted lab report will be given back to you with comments and graded with CO or H. If you be given an H-grade you must re-submit the report one week after hand back.

 

Safety

Students must wear appropriate footwear. Tongs or sandals are not accepted. Use extreme care in performing all experiments. If you observe or feel that unsafe working conditions exist, inform the lecturer immediately.

 

Laboratory Rules

Students are expected to clean up everything directly related to their work. Equipment used for tests must be cleaned, washed, wiped off, etc. as required to return it to its original condition. Put in order as you found it for locking away by the lab technician. Do not flush soils or other matter down sinks; dispose it in bins!

Table of Contents

The table of contents should follow the cover letter. It should include report section titles, figures, and appendices with their corresponding page numbers.

(Use the style headings in MS-Word to insert the contents page in your report)

 

Procedure

Type up the procedure used to perform the test.. This is also an appropriate place for a description of the soil you tested.

 

Results

Results consist of graphs, figures, and/or tables that present your findings in a concise manner. Briefly describes what you are presenting in each of your graphs, figures, and/or tables.

All graphs figures, and tables should have a title.

 

Sample Calculations

Sample calculations must be included to show exactly what calculations you performed in order to obtain your results. They include numeric examples. Show a sample calculation for only one of the data points. It is important to include these in the body of your report not in an appendix. [MS-Word has an equation editor which can be easily access (Insert- Object and look for Microsoft Equation)].

 

Discussion

The discussion should be as concise as possible. The discussion interprets the results obtained in the experiment, and evaluates whether or not the objectives outlined in the introduction were met. Do the results agree with known values? If not, why not? What are the shortcomings and strengths of the results?

 

References

Any materials that you reference need to be cited. This is very important.

 

Appendices

If you have extensive data or a long calculation that may be of interest to someone reading the report, but is not necessarily crucial for the experiment then an appendix is appropriate.
