Assignment Submission Requirements

An assignment submission form, including cover page with a plagiarism declaration is required for each assignment. Assignments to be handed in as loose paper collection stapled together at the top left corner. The assignment should be presented as a technical report. It must consist of a cover page, table of contents, and should have an introduction, a body, a conclusion or recommendation, and a references page.

Do not forget to number the pages. Any embellishment, fancy fonts and borders should be avoided. (Your report is a technical document and not a selling brochure that needs to catch the attention of customer.)

Include photos, diagrams sketches, tables etc., and do not forget to refer to them in your text.

Do your research, and then write up a draft. Before you write your final assignment, you may discuss your draft with your lecturer to clear up any problems you may have encountered. Make sure your final assignment is ready by the due date.

If you have difficulties or are unfamiliar with report writing, contact the Learning Resource Centre (LRC) where you will find numerous textbooks about report writing.

To complete the subject assessment satisfactorily two assignments and two presentations are required.

Oral presentation requirements

It is important to plan your presentation. Approach your lecturer if you need some guidance on this matter. The most difficult part is keeping your presentation within the time limit of 10 minutes. Refer to ‘Oral Presentation’.

Assessments

Each of the two assignments and oral presentations will count 25% and 25% respectively towards your final marks. A pass mark of 60% for each assignment and 50% for each of the oral presentation is required. Assignments should be submitted and the oral presentation must be given on the due date. Students who do not turn up and fail to give their talk on the stipulated day cannot complete this subject and need to re-enroll. Assignments submitted up to a week later than the due date will receive a reduction in marks (5% for each day). Assignments will not be accepted later than seven (7) days late. Therefore make sure you submit your assignment on the date when it is due. All students must turn up for the presentation. Students who are not present at oral presentations lose 5 marks.
