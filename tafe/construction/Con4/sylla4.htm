MODULE TITLE                        CONSTRUCTION 4

 

Nominal duration                          35- 40 hours

 

National                                       ABCO04

 

Discipline code                              1003105

 

Module purpose                         To provide students with knowledge of construction principles, standards and services commonly used in commercial and residential buildings up to an effective height of 25m.

Delivery of the module              This module can be delivered in any way, which allows students to acquire the skills and knowledge necessary to achieve the learning outcomes.

 

Assessment method                   A selection of the following methods can be used: Written and photographic reports

                                                    Short answer tests

                                                    Drafted or freehand construction details

                                                    Construction models

 

Conditions of assessment          It is assumed that most of the training related to this module will be off the job.

 

                                                    Learners will be required to demonstrate safe work practices.

 

                                                    Learners will be provided with a list of possible sources of information and references.

 

                                                    During assessment students shall have access to permitted references.

 

 

 

Learning outcomes                    On completion of this module the student will be able to satisfy the following learning outcomes:

 

Learning outcome 1                   Investigate, interpret and communicate to others the effects that Local Government and Service Supply Authorities regulations, the Building Code of Australia and relevant Australian Standards have on the design and construction of commercial and residential buildings.

 

 

Learning outcome 2                   Describe in detail the types, characteristics, classification, behaviour and effects of foundations as well as the role of a soil engineer in foundation assessment and selection of footing systems.

 

Learning outcome 3                   Describe the principles, standard practices and safety requirements of site establishment, builders' plant and equipment, storage and scaffolding.

 

 

Learning outcome 4                   Describe in detail the principles, safety requirements and standard practices of demolition and site preparation.

 

Learning outcome 5                   Describe and sketch types, principles, construction practices of shoring, underpinning, de-watering, ground stabilisation, footing systems and basement construction.

 

Learning outcome 6                   Describe in detail the types, principles, FRLs, sound transmission, construction standards and practices of these structural systems as commonly used in commercial and residential buildings:

 

 

Learning outcome 7                   Describe in detail the types, principles, FRLs, sound transmission, application/installation, standards and practices of suspended and applied ceilings, ducted floors systems, light and heavy weight partition systems.

 

 

Learning outcome 8                   Describe the types, principles, application methods and standard practices of external and internal finishes and coatings to floors, walls, ceilings appropriate to commercial and residential buildings.

 

Learning outcome 9                   Demonstrate knowledge of the types, principles, relevant to BCA regulations, construction standards and practices related to the design and construction of stairs, balustrading and handrails for commercial and residential buildings.

 

Learning outcome 10                 Demonstrate a knowledge of the types, materials, finishes, fabrication, installation, and standards related to:

·        Commercial windows.

·        Commercial garage doors.

·        Metal doorframes.

·        Fire doors.

 

Learning outcome 11                 Demonstrate a knowledge of the types, principles, installation standards, practices and co-ordination of natural and mechanical ventilation as well as air- conditioning including associated plant and air-handling systems, all related to commercial and residential building

 

Learning outcome 12                 Demonstrate a knowledge of the types, construction standards and practices of site works associated with commercial and residential buildings:

·        Stormwater drainage

·        Paving

·        Retaining walls.

 

Learning outcome 13                 Demonstrate a knowledge of the current range of viable vertical transportation solutions.

 

 

Learning outcome 14                 Describe construction details for commercial residential buildings to an effective height of 25m.
