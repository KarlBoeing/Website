MODULE TITLE                  Builders' Working Drawings 2

 

Nominal duration                   35 - 40 hours

 

Module codes

            DETAFE(SA)             CDAG

            National                      ABC062

 

Discipline code                       1001110

 

Module purpose                     Further develop the students skills in plan and specification reading and interpretation, and drafting and sketching skills for low rise residential buildings.

 

Relationship to competency standards

                                                No Competency Standards are currently available.

 

Pre-requisites                         Builders' Working Drawings 1

 

Co-requisites                          Construction 1

 

Summary of content               Read plans

                                                Sketch building components

                                                Prepare detailed drawings.

 

Delivery of the module          This module can be delivered in any way which allows students to acquire the skills and knowledge necessary to achieve the learning outcomes.

 

Assessment method               Drawing projects, pictorial sketches and sections of construction and finishing components, together with a final folio of working drawings and specifications notes.

 

Conditions of assessment      It is assumed that most of the training related to this module will be off the job.

                                                Learners will be required to demonstrate safe work practices.

                                                Learners will be provided with a list of possible sources of information and references.

                                                During assessment students shall have access to permitted references.

 

Learning outcomes                On completion of this module the learner will be able to:

 

Learning outcome 1               To read and interpret plans, specifications, structural engineers' reports, soil tests, survey plans and levels, used by the builder in the construction of two storey residential buildings.

 

Assessment criteria               1.1 Primary - Building construction detailing or building common sense - " Build ability"

                                                1.2 Secondary - The ability to communicate this by drawing using assessment considerations that may include:

·              drawing ability and presentation

·              compliance with Codes, Standards and Regulations

·              creativity, evidence of independent research

·              content and effort

·              addressing the set topic

·              use of set texts, class notes etc

·              use of site visits, photographs and directed research

 

Learning outcome 2               Draw three dimensional sketches.

 

Assessment criteria               2.1 Produce annotated 3D sketches of various building components using parametric (eg isometric) or perspective techniques.

 

Learning outcome 3               Draw a partial set of selected working drawings with building details and associated specification notes, suitable for building permit approval.

 

Assessment criteria               3.1 Demonstrate a knowledge, understanding and drawing ability by the satisfactory completion of a set of selected building details with specification notes for a double storey dwelling, from the following list:

·              Site Plans

·              Land surveyors plans, levels, contours

·              Certificate of Title to land

·              Excavation, cut and fill

·              Retaining walls, banks, landscaping

·              Sewerage connection, easements - plan

·              Stormwater connection, easements - plan

·              Electrical connections - plan

·              Soil classification, soil tests

·              Base structure - timber and masonry

·              Wall construction - timber and masonry

·              Internal and external wall claddings

·              Roof construction

·              Upper floor construction

·              Chimney construction

·              Composite construction eg. steel and timber

·              Complex roof and wall shapes, flashings box gutters

·              Stairs

·              Glazing, including bay window construction

·              Window and door schedules

·              Insulation, sarking

·              Cathedral ceilings

·              Large span timber beams and connections (including glue laminated beams)

·              Joinery

·              Reading of plans and specifications and structural engineers and conversion to architectural/building detail

·              Application of relevant Codes, Regulations and Standards

·              Content relates particularly to double storey dwellings.

 

Suggested learning resources          

                                                State Building Regulations Incorporating Building Code of Australia.

                                                AS3700 Masonry Code.

                                                AS1100 Architectural Drawing and supplement

                                                AS2870 Residential Slabs and Footings Code.

                                                AS1684 National Timber Framing Code and

                                                Supplements

                                                Other relevant standards and Government Acts and

                                                Regulations.

                                                Guest lecturers, eg. Structural Engineers, Land Surveyors,

                                                Building Surveyors etc.
