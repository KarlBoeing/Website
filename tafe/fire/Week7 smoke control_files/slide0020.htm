The objectives of smoke control
The need to control smoke in a fire seems obvious and the BCA requires that occupants of buildings be safeguarded in a variety of ways from the effects of smoke and its potential to hinder their escape.
The objectives of smoke control should encompass:
• the maintenance of escape paths free of smoke
• control of the spread of smoke
• the reduction of smoke that may hinder the fire- fighter’s role
• the reduction of smoke damage to other parts of a building affected by fire.
