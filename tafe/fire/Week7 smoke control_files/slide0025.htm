The mechanism of smoke movement
The main factors that determine the movement of smoke and hot gases from a fire in a building are:
• the smoke’s own mobility, or buoyancy,
• the normal air movement inside the building,
The movement caused by the smoke’s mobility, or buoyancy is due to pressure differentials created by:
• the expansion of the gases as they are heated by the fire
• the difference in density between the hot gases and hot air above the   flames and the cooler air surrounding the fire.
The normal movement of air in the building is usually caused by the following
factors:
• the stack effect,
• the wind outside;
• the presence, or otherwise, of a mechanical air-handling system or   mechanical ventilators inside the building.
