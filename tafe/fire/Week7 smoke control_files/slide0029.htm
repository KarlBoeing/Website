Air locks
Air locks or smoke lobbies prevent, or at least to reduce considerably, the entry of smoke into the defined fire-emergency exit system. When air locks protect the entrances to fire-isolated stairs, they may be arranged as:
• lobby-approach stair
• ventilated lobby-approach stair
• balcony-approach stair
• isolated tower stair.
Smoke lobbies are used as both natural and mechanical means for smoke control. The benefit is that in order to reach the exit system, the smoke has to pass through two sets of doors, across each of which a pressure opposing the smoke flow can be developed.
