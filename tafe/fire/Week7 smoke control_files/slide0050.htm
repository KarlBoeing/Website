Spread of Flame Index
The spread of flame index is based on studies of actual rates of spread of flame on various wall-lining materials.
Where the walls of rooms with 2.75m ceiling height were lined with materials with high spread of flame indices, flames rapidly spread up the wall and, by igniting combustible gases that had accumulated below the ceiling, rapidly involved the whole room in fire.
An index of 10 indicates that the material could be expected to cause flames to reach the ceiling of such a room within 10s of ignition; an index of zero means that the materials will not cause flames to reach the ceiling.
