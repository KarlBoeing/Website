Smoke Developed Index
The smoke developed index relates to the optical density of smoke produced under the conditions of the standard test.
Doubling of the optical density of the smoke increases the smoke developed index. The higher the index, the greater the hazard is likely to be from smoke.
The thickness of the material can affect the smoke produced as can the mass to surface area ratio.
Where materials with different ignition behaviour are in combination, the smoke produced can be affected by the ignition behaviour of individual components. Most materials smoke before ignition but a few produce more smoke after ignition.
