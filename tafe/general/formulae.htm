UNITS

Without Units any calculations are meaningless. An answer without units is a wrong answer. To demonstrate the important of units consider the following example:

If someone borrows from you $10 and later repays you with 10 cents
you would not be pleased, because 10 cents are much less worth than $10.

Although the figure 10 in both cases occur and this is correct you will definitely agree that without units your answer will be senseless.

It is important to think about unit before you start your calculation.

All units in equations must be compatible. Whatever unit you may select, you must make sure that this unit will be used throughout your calculation. (You cannot multiply meter by millimetre)
