BUILDING SITE SURVEY AND SET OUT 2

PREREQUISITES:   Building Site Surveying and Set Out 1

INSTRUCTIONS TO STUDENTS:

The aim of this module is to extend the students knowledge of basic measuring and levelling techniques to include instruments and calculations necessary in the set out and control of large residential developments, commercial and industrial construction.

a)   This module is designed to be carried out over one Semester at 1.5 hours each class.

b)   The class will be divided into groups of three or four depending on the scope of work.

c)   Your task is based on a TEAM effort and it is the responsibility of each student in the group to       maintain this perspective (refer to key competencies).

TASK:


Future development at eastern site of the Leederville Campus requires an “as built” plan (site plan with the exact location of all buildings including RL’s as shown below.

Figure 1

Each group of students is to carry out a comprehensive site survey of one (1) designated area as nominated by their lecturer. There are two given Site Bench marks to which all levels should be related.

PROJECTS - Scope of Work


Groups must draw up:
·        a detailed site plan scale 1:200, of their designated area
·        elevations scale 1:100 (E & C Block) and 1:200 (B Block)


Group A - E Block area
Agree on the extent of your work with Group B

Group B - B Block area
Agree on the extent of your work with Group A & C

Group C - C Block area
Agree on the extent of your work with Group B

Overlapping and reference aligning marks are important for the final production of your master plan. (advantage would be using CAD).



Figure 2

Accurately measure and/or plot the layout of all:
·        Buildings indicating all RL as directed by the lecturer
·        Height of buildings (for drawing elevations)
·        Access to buildings (stairs ramps etc)
·        FFL’s of buildings
·        Curbs, Driveways and Paths
·        Retaining walls (RL’s on top of wall and lower & higher side of wall)
·        Existing visible underground services
·        Landscaped areas with major trees (diameter Æ 150 mm)
·        Contour interval generally 0.2 metres (for smaller height difference 0.1 metres) and slopes of      driveways
 ·        Fences (indicate brick, fibro, mesh etc)

The theodolite will be used for all height measurements necessary for drawing elevations.

The site plan and elevations of each group is to be plotted onto an A2-size tracing sheet to display all features in ink. Each drawing must have a title block with all section filled in.

All Groups are responsible for the preparation of the final master plan. The final master plan is a plan of all single group plans combined into one at a scale of 1:500.

Copies and the original transparencies are to be presented and handed in to the lecturer by the due date.

Each group is responsible for the booking of all relevant equipment required for the surveying task. All gear must be returned to the store room (Locke room access No CZ160).

Safety regulations must be followed at all times. Safety vests and proper footwear are to be worn for all field-work. Safety signs (Surveyor on Road) are required if you work in trafficable areas.
