Material properties (stress and strain

Definition:

Stress:   gives the intensity of the internal reaction force in a material created by an external loading. It is measured as the load acting per unit area.

Strain:    or deformation is the change in shape of a body caused by an external loading. Strain is the ratio of the change in length per unit length.

1.     Draw a force/extension (stress/strain) diagram for a low carbon steel on graph paper and nominate the following:

a) permissible stress          b) yield point      c) ultimate stress
  d) elastic range              e) plastic range     f) proportional limit
          

2.     Define the terms ductile  and brittle and explain briefly the difference between a ductile and brittle material.


3.      Calculate the stress in the following members:
         a) Timber post cross-sectional size 100 x 100 mm with a 50 kN loading.
         b) Steel bar diameter 24 mm subjected to a tensile load of 8.1 ton.

4.      A tensile force of 120 kN has been applied to a steel bar of 12 mm diameter. The original length of the bar is 13 metres and under the force of 120kN the bar  extended to a length of 13065 mm. Calculate the strain.
