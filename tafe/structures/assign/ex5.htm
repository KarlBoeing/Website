Assignment 5

Dead & Live load calculations

Question 1

Calculate the weight of the following structural member:

  1. Timber beam 300 x 150 mm, 6.5 metres long.

  2. Brickwall 5.2 metres long, 2.4 metres high and 110 mm thick.

  3. Concrete slab 172 mm thick, 5.8 metres long and 3.6 metres wide.

Density:
Timber 900 kg/m3, Brickwork 1.9 t/m3, Conrete 2.5 t/m3.


Question 2

Pre-cast concrete slabs are supported by three beam as shown in the Figure 1 below. The slabs are each 1.2 metres wide, 150 mm thick and 3.6 metres and 4.8 m long. A 50 mm concrete screed is laid on top of the slabs.


Figure 1

The following must be calculated:

  1. the weight of each of the 3.6 m and 4.8 m long slabs.
  2. the dead load (slabs and screed) on Beam 1, Beam 2 and Beam 3,
    beam length 4.8 m.
  3. the live load on Beam 1, Beam 2 and Beam 3 (LL 3 kPa).
The density for the pre-cast elements is 2500 kg/m3 and for the conrete screed is 2300 kg/m3


Question 3

Determine the weight of a 250 x 250 mm concrete column 6 metres long, reinforced with eight 24 mm diameter reinforcing bars.

Density for the concrete is 2300 kg/m3 and for the steel is 7850 kg/m3
