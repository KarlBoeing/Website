The Modulus of Elasticity or Young's Modulus, (E), is a measure of the resistance of an elastic material to being stretched or shortened. Elastic materials obey Hooke's Law, which states that stress is proportional to strain, provided the elastic limit of the material is not exceeded. Shortening or lengthening of the material will be less the greater the value of E is. E is needed for the calculation of deflections.

UNITS

Without Units any calculations are meaningless. An answer without units is a wrong answer. To demonstrate the important of units consider the following example:

If someone borrows from you $10 and later repays you with 10 cents you would not be pleased, because 10 cents are much less worth than $10.

Although the figure 10 in both cases occur and if this is correct you will definitely agree that without units your answer will be senseless.
It is important to think about unit before you start your calculation. All units in equations must be compatible. Whatever unit you may select, you must make sure that this unit will be used throughout your calculation. (You cannot multiply meter by millimetre)
